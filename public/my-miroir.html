My Miroir

Butoh, Opera, and Rewriting the Truth

This mirror cracks open everything society told me to hide or erase. Every move, every note, and every breath here is a piece of that rebellion. I dance to heal the souls and to shatter the quiet that suffocates us. I sing to bring back what fear tried to bury deep inside us all.
Inside Miroir, you will see what society tries to erase: Truth, scars, and I hope it will awaken the souls that are still sleeping.
Carmen In this operatic performance, I embody the seduction of power and the punishment of desire. Carmen has always been misunderstood. I let her live in my own voice and in the haunting fire of rebellion.

Reclaiming Lost Childhood This Butoh was born from grief, the moments stolen by trauma, by shame, by forced conformity. I present how society wanted to erase my real identity by punishing, mocking, and bullying me, but I refused to be caged.

O Fortuna Here, I surrender to the forces of fate, screaming through movement that I was never meant to be quiet. It is also a stormy and unfiltered truth exposing existing moral hypocrisy.

Der Holle Through this I visit the darkness society builds around femme bodies, queer souls, and sacred rage. The underworld is not hell. It is truth unleashed.

Lacrimosa My weeping body in this Butoh channels the grief of exile. From churches, families, and nations. Every tear here carries a hymn.
