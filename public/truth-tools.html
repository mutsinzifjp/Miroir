A Survival Kit for Unfiltered Creators

[For creators who refuse to be reshaped by silence, fear, or algorithms.]

They can hide your post. They can ban your account, but if your voice is rooted in purpose, not provocation, it will always find a way to be heard.

Dear Truth-Telling Creators,

We live in a world that often prefers polite lies over bold honesty, a world where being agreeable is more celebrated than being awake. In such a world, those who dare to speak up, to name what is unjust or buried, are met not with applause but suspicion. We are taught to soften our voices while our communities burn, to be likable instead of truthful.

That is why I created this survival guide for creators who don’t post for claps but to awaken. This is not a goldmine; it is a mirror, a companion for those ready to dig with their bare hands and speak truths that aren’t always trending.

When I started sharing my truth online, I was my own audience. I liked my own posts, I hesitated with every word, editing and re-editing to not offend. I tried pleasing, I tried silence, but silence never saved me. So I just chose truth over perfection or applause.

I had no team, I had no tech background, but I built this space by asking questions to ChatGPT, dragging shapes in Canva, and refusing to vanish. You don’t need to be a genius to build something real; you just need to keep going, even if it’s barefoot.

What You’ll Find Here

  • The best free tools to create fearlessly (even with no budget)
  • How to plan content that avoids visibility suppression
  • Backup strategies in case your content is removed
  • How to stay honest without putting yourself in harm’s way
  • Ways to use online platforms with clarity and caution

This is also a collaboration. I’ve included an open form where you can offer your own insights, because this isn’t just my voice, it’s our shared breath.

Invisibility Is the New Censorship

I’ve seen creators get their accounts flagged or quietly de-ranked, not because they spread harm, but because they dared to speak from lived pain. I’ve seen poetic posts labeled as violations, art mistaken for hate, and honest terms mistaken for danger. Here are real examples:

  • In 2021, Instagram and Twitter removed hundreds of #SaveSheikhJarrah posts (Al Jazeera)
  • Feminist and decolonial resistance content were flagged and removed by the Instagram and TikTok algorithms (Time, Sage)
  • Queer creators using words like “femme” or “nonbinary” were suppressed(TeenVogue, them)

How to Stay Loud and Smart

Some of us call a spade a spade, some of us name injustice directly, but we must also recognize: not everyone you speak for will stand beside you once you’re targeted. So here are strategies to speak the truth while staying safe:

  • Use metaphor, poetry, or art to veil bold messages
  • Replace rants with symbolic visuals
  • Avoid flagged hashtags
  • Re-upload your work across platforms
  • Download your content weekly

Some of the Tools I Recommend

  • Archive.org for mirroring your truth in public records.
  • Notion for journaling and drafts.
  • USB/Drives for offline safety.
  • Your own domain name.

My Toolbox

  • ChatGPT for questions, code, ideas
  • Adobe Express for easy, and faster animated contents
  • Canva for design and story visuals
  • Tally.so for open forms and collaboration
  • Vercel + GitHub: For free website hosting.

Publishing Platforms

You don’t need a publisher to tell your truth; you can publish your book yourself and share your work in ways no algorithm can bury.

  • OSF, Zenodo and other preprint servers for self-publishing with a DOI
  • Amazon KDP for books
  • Substack

A World Colonized Without Chains

We live in a world where chains no longer jingle, they shimmer in ad banners, trends, in likes, or even silence. This is a world where:

  • Profit often outweighs people
  • The truth is framed as an inconvenience
  • People silence themselves before being silenced

So when they tell you you’re too much, label you as angry, intense, or ungrateful, remember: that might be the moment your truth struck deep. You don’t need to edit your fire, and you don’t need to sweeten your voice. They told me to tone it down, spiritualize it, and make it digestible, and I chose to unfilter and speak where I was once silent. Let your content say what your society refuses to hear.
